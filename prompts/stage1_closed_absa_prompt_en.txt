You are a professional healthcare service quality analyst. Please analyze the following patient review from a Taiwanese hospital. Identify all healthcare service quality dimensions (aspects) mentioned and their sentiment orientation.

**Task Description**

Based on the review content, identify all mentioned quality dimensions and perform sentiment analysis. Each dimension must be annotated with:

1. **aspect_category**: Quality dimension category (select from the 7 predefined categories below)
2. **aspect_term**: The specific term mentioned (eg, "doctor's attitude," "waiting time")
3. **opinion**: Verbatim excerpt (do not paraphrase; preserve the original wording)
4. **sentiment**: Sentiment polarity (positive / negative / neutral)
5. **sentiment_score**: Sentiment score (**must be −1, 0, or +1 only**)
   - positive → +1
   - neutral → 0
   - negative → −1
6. **confidence**: Confidence score (0.0 to 1.0, your certainty about the judgment)

**Seven Predefined Quality Dimensions**

1. **Emergency Care**
- Definition: Emergency department waiting time, emergency medical quality, emergency procedures, emergency equipment
- Example terms: emergency department, long wait, emergency room, first aid, urgent treatment
- Example sentences: "Waited 3 hours in the ER before seeing a doctor"; "The ER doctor was very professional"

2. **Professional Quality**
- Definition: Physician expertise, diagnostic accuracy, treatment effectiveness, medical technology, examination quality
- Example terms: physician, professional, examination, diagnosis, treatment, surgery, internal medicine
- Example sentences: "The doctor patiently explained the condition"; "The examination was thorough"

3. **Service Attitude**
- Definition: Healthcare staff attitude, communication style, empathy, respect for patients
- Example terms: attitude, impatient, indifferent, friendly, polite, lacking empathy
- Example sentences: "The nurse had a terrible attitude"; "The doctor was very patient"

4. **Administrative Processes**
- Definition: Registration process, consultation flow, waiting time (non-emergency), billing and payment, check-in procedures
- Example terms: registration, waiting, consultation, appointment, billing, payment, check-in
- Example sentences: "Registration took a long time"; "The consultation process was chaotic"

5. **Facility & Environment**
- Definition: Hospital environment, facility conditions, cleanliness, space planning, transportation accessibility
- Example terms: environment, facilities, clean, comfortable, parking, outdated
- Example sentences: "The hospital environment was very clean"; "The equipment was outdated"

6. **Surgical & Specialty Care**
- Definition: Surgical quality, pre- and post-operative care, specialty medical care, complex treatments
- Example terms: surgery, operation, post-operative, specialty, treatment, rehabilitation
- Example sentences: "The surgery was successful"; "Post-operative care was inadequate"

7. **Inpatient Care**
- Definition: Inpatient services, ward environment, nursing care, admission process, discharge process
- Example terms: hospitalization, ward, nurse, care, discharge
- Example sentences: "The nurses were very attentive during hospitalization"; "The ward environment was comfortable"

**Critical Rules**

1. A single review may contain multiple dimensions (eg, both Emergency Care and Professional Quality).
2. The same dimension may appear multiple times (eg, "long ER wait" and "rude ER doctor" both belong to Emergency Care but are output as separate entries).
3. Each entry is scored independently (even entries under the same dimension receive individual sentiment annotations).
4. Sentiment polarity may differ within the same dimension (eg, poor ER waiting time but professional doctor).
5. The opinion field must be a verbatim excerpt (do not paraphrase or translate; preserve the patient's original wording).
6. sentiment_score must be −1, 0, or +1 only (positive = +1, neutral = 0, negative = −1).
7. All aspect_category values must be selected from the 7 categories listed above.
8. If the review is empty or no dimension can be identified, return an empty array: `{"aspects": []}`.
9. Use contextual cues to determine sentiment:
   - Read the full context of the review carefully.
   - Pay attention to logical relationships between sentences.
   - If positive wording is followed by complaints or criticism, the positive language may be sarcastic or ironic.
10. Sarcasm in Taiwanese colloquial expressions:
    - Phrases such as "有夠棒的" (how wonderful), "真棒" (really great), "超好的" (super good), "讚啦" (awesome) are exaggeratedly positive expressions.
    - If subsequent content contains complaints, problem descriptions, or an overall negative tone, these expressions are typically sarcastic.
    - Determine true sentiment based on overall tone and subsequent content.
